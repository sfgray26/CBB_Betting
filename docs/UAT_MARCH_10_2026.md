## Momentum Analysis

### 1. Hidden Momentum (Not obvious from seed line)
*   **Cincinnati Bearcats:** While their record is 7-3 in the last 10, they rank **Top 10 nationally in 3P% since February 1**. Their perimeter shooting has transformed them from a bubble team to a dangerous high-major out. They secured a first-round Big 12 win over Utah and are "shooting the lights out" entering March.
*   **Santa Clara Broncos:** Their momentum is peaked after a massive upset of co-champion Saint Mary's in the WCC semifinals. They are playing for their first NCAA bid since 1996 tonight. Their defensive intensity has spiked in the last three games, holding opponents 12% below their season scoring averages.

### 2. Misleading Momentum (Underlying stats declined)
*   **Duke Blue Devils:** On paper, an 8-2 record and No. 1 Defensive Efficiency looks elite. However, they lost star guard **Caleb Foster** to a fractured foot on March 7 and have **Patrick Ngongba II** sidelined for the ACC tournament. Their "momentum" is physically compromised, and their offensive flow has regressed without Foster's playmaking.
*   **Kansas Jayhawks:** Though they are a top talent, they have dropped 4 of their last 6 games. Star freshman **Darryn Peterson** is playing through multiple nagging injuries (hamstring/ankle) and persistent cramping. Their efficiency metrics have dipped from Top 5 to Top 25 in the last three weeks, making their "blue blood" status misleading relative to current form.

### 3. Efficiency Flags
*   **Akron (MAC):** Flagged for the most significant defensive improvement in the country over the last 30 days, jumping 150 spots in efficiency rankings.
*   **Arizona:** Entering the tournament with the No. 3 defensive efficiency and a 9-1 stretch, making them the "truest" powerhouse momentum team alongside Michigan.

## Fantasy Strategy & Context

### 1. "Buy Low" Candidates (Velo Up, ADP Suppressed)
*   **Bryce Miller (SEA) - ADP ~240:** After a disappointing, injury-riddled 2025 (5.68 ERA), Miller has returned with a +2.1 mph jump on his heater. He is sitting 97-98 mph with his signature "ride." The market is still pricing in his 2025 struggles, making him a premier post-200 value.
*   **Ryan Weathers (NYY) - ADP ~235:** The move to the Yankees and a reported mechanical tweak in the offseason has his velocity touching triple digits. With Cole and Rodón starting 2026 on the IL, Weathers has a clear path to high-leverage innings in a pitcher-friendly park (relative to his 2024 Miami stint).
*   **Keider Montero (DET) - ADP >500:** Deep sleeper. He was optioned to Triple-A on March 4, which has completely removed him from redraft boards. However, his +2.0 mph gain makes him the first man up for a Tigers rotation that frequently deals with health issues.

### 2. "Fade" Candidates (Velo Down, ADP Too High)
*   **Sean Manaea (NYM) - ADP ~314:** While 314 isn't "high," any investment is risky with a 4.3 mph drop. He is trying to integrate a cutter and a lower arm slot, which has zapped his sinker's effectiveness. He averaged a career-high 181.2 IP in 2024; his arm may be showing fatigue.
*   **Carlos Estévez (KCR):** Though ADP is lower for relievers, an 8 mph drop is catastrophic for a pitcher whose profile depends on "high cheese." Even if he recovers 3-4 mph, he'll be sitting at 92-93 mph, well below his 2024 closing form.

### 3. Mechanical & Injury Context
*   **Dustin May (STL):** Averaging 97.8 mph (up from 95.4 in 2025). He is finally showing the pre-esophageal tear form from 2023.
*   **Walker Buehler (SDP):** Sitting at 93.8 mph (down 1.2 mph). He is shifting to a "crafty" approach (more sweepers/cutters) as the 95-97 mph gas hasn't fully returned post-TJ.
*   **Shota Imanaga (CHC):** Showing a +1.3 mph increase (93.0 mph). He recovered from 2025 hamstring issues and has refined his "rising" four-seamer.
